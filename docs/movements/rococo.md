# Rococo

**Period:** 1700-1770

## Style

- aristocratic pleasure after the heavy Baroque — lightness, flirtation, pastel
- fête galante: elegant couples in park settings
- asymmetric shell (rocaille) and scroll ornament
- pale pastel palette — sky blue, rose, mint, cream, dove grey
- feathery brushwork, airy compositions
- mythology as playful pretext for nudes and idyll, not moral lesson
- interior decoration integrated with painting (Paris hôtels, German palaces)

## Key artists

- Antoine Watteau
- François Boucher
- Jean-Honoré Fragonard
- Giovanni Battista Tiepolo
- Jean-Siméon Chardin (countercurrent — sober middle-class still life)
- William Hogarth (English satirical counterpart)
- Thomas Gainsborough
- Joshua Reynolds
- Watteau
- Boucher

## Techniques

- pastel palette with deliberately high-key values
- dabs of pure color to suggest silk sheen
- S-curve and asymmetric composition
- sketchy, feathery foliage with lacy silhouettes
- pastel (dry medium) portraiture alongside oil
- ceiling fresco dissolving upward into pink sunlit clouds (Tiepolo)
- shimmering loose brushwork
- stucco with gilded shell and leaf motifs
