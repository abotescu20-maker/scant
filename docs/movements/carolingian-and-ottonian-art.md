# Carolingian and Ottonian art

**Period:** c.800-1050 AD

## Style

- conscious revival of Roman models
- agitated nervous line in drawing (Utrecht Psalter)
- monumental bronze doors revived (Hildesheim)
- strong gestural figures with wide eyes
- return of narrative relief
- patronage by emperor and court
- illusionistic landscape hints recovered from antique sources

## Key artists

- anonymous
- Bishop Bernward of Hildesheim (patron)

## Techniques

- bronze casting of doors
- pen-and-ink drawing on vellum
- ivory carving
- [[manuscript-illumination|manuscript illumination]]
- gold and enamel book covers
