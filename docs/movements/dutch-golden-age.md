# Dutch Golden Age

**Period:** 1600-1700

## Style

- Protestant merchant republic — no church/royal commissions, open market
- specialization: each painter a specialist in one genre (seascape, still-life, church interior, tavern scene, portrait)
- 'mirror of nature' — everyday middle-class life rendered with reverent precision
- landscape with low horizon, vast grey-silver skies taking two-thirds of canvas
- genre painting (domestic interiors, taverns) as new subject category
- quiet light from a window as moral/spiritual presence
- vanitas still-life: skulls, snuffed candles, wilting flowers as memento mori

## Techniques

- Rembrandt: golden glow against deep brown darkness, thick impasto on lit passages, thin glazes in shadow, etched lines with drypoint burr
- Vermeer: cool daylight from left, pearl-like scattered highlights (pointillé), pigment build-up on glinting objects, camera obscura-aided drawing
- Hals: rapid 'alla prima' wet-in-wet strokes visible and unblended, capturing laughter and momentary expression
- Ruisdael: towering cloud architecture, single shaft of sun on a ruin or windmill
- Saenredam: precise architectural perspective of whitewashed church interiors
- tonal (monochrome) landscape: silver-grey-ochre atmospheric unity
