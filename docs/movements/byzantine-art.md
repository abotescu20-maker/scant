# Byzantine art

**Period:** c.500-1453 AD

## Style

- gold ground (divine timeless space)
- hieratic frontal figures
- elongated bodies, small feet
- large staring almond-shaped eyes
- reverse perspective (objects widen toward viewer)
- strict iconographic canon handed down by monastic tradition
- rich jewel-like mosaic tesserae set at angles to catch light
- linear highlights on drapery in gold lines (chrysography)

## Key artists

- anonymous (mostly monks)
- the 'Constantinople school'

## Techniques

- gold-ground wall mosaic
- tempera on wood panel (icon)
- manuscript illumination with gold leaf
- cloisonné enamel
- [[chrysography|chrysography]]
