# Ligne claire

**Period:** c. 1929-present (term coined 1977)

## Overview

Ligne claire ("clear line") is the signature style of Franco-Belgian bande dessinée developed by Georges Remi ([[herge|Hergé]]) for *The Adventures of Tintin*, first serialised in *Le Petit Vingtième* (1929). The term was coined by Dutch comics artist [[joost-swarte|Joost Swarte]] in 1977 for an exhibition at Rotterdam's Museum Boijmans Van Beuningen. The style combines strong, uniform contour lines with flat, unshaded colour fills and meticulous documentary detail, producing images that are simultaneously cartoon-simplified and hyper-legible.

## Technique

- uniform-weight black contour line drawn with technical pens or fine brushes
- no hatching, crosshatching, or feathered shadows within forms
- flat colour fills (applied via coloured overlays at print stage)
- characters stylised toward geometric simplicity; settings rendered with documentary precision
- clear compositional hierarchy: every object legible in silhouette
- consistent lighting — no chiaroscuro, rare cast shadows

## Key practitioners and works

- [[herge|Hergé (Georges Remi)]] — *Tintin au pays des Soviets* (1929) through *Tintin et les Picaros* (1976)
- Edgar P. Jacobs — *Blake and Mortimer* series (from 1946)
- Bob de Moor, Jacques Martin — Studios Hergé associates
- [[yves-chaland|Yves Chaland]] — *Freddy Lombard* (1981-90), revival of the style
- [[joost-swarte|Joost Swarte]] — Dutch cartoonist who named the movement
- Ted Benoit, Floc'h, Serge Clerc — atomic-age ligne claire revival
- Jason Lutes — *Berlin* (1996-2018)

## Visual signatures for AI

- [[uniform-line-weight|uniform line weight]] throughout
- [[flat-color-fills|flat colour fills]] with no gradient, no rendering
- [[clear-line-composition|clear line composition]] — every element reads cleanly
- European locations, 1930s-60s period detail (cars, trains, architecture)
- rounded-face characters with dot-and-dash eyes (Hergé facial grammar)
- clean gutters and regular grid page layout
- saturated but limited palette; primaries plus earth tones

## Relations

- [[cyberpunk|Cyberpunk]] — [[mobius|Mœbius]] hybridised ligne claire with sci-fi
- Art Nouveau poster design (Cassandre) — flat-colour ancestor
- [[herge|Hergé]], [[yves-chaland|Yves Chaland]], [[joost-swarte|Joost Swarte]]
