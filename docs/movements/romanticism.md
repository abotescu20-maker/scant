# Romanticism

**Period:** 1800-1850

## Style

- exaltation of individual emotion, imagination, the sublime
- nature as awesome, terrifying, indifferent — storms, Alps, shipwrecks
- exotic and orientalist subjects (North Africa, medieval chivalry)
- historical catastrophe as subject (Raft of the Medusa, Massacre at Chios)
- colour over line — Delacroix vs Ingres debate
- visible expressive brushwork and saturated colour as carriers of feeling
- the artist-genius as outsider-prophet
- ruins, graveyards, moonlight as charged motifs

## Techniques

- Goya: velvety black grounds, loose blood-red and ochre slashes, aquatint etching for the Caprichos/Disasters
- Delacroix: complementary colour pairs (orange against blue) vibrated side by side; rapid broken brushwork
- Turner: dissolved form into tornado vortices of yellow, white, and grey; thin washes and thick white impasto on lit passages; watercolour techniques carried into oil
- Constable: broken 'Constable snow' — flecks of pure white to render dew and sparkle; oil sketches done outdoors directly from motif
- Friedrich: silhouetted Rückenfigur (figure seen from behind) gazing into sublime landscape; luminous low horizon fog
- Géricault: pyramidal composition of bodies; morbid realism from morgue studies
- agitated diagonals and dramatic contrasts of light/dark
- visible, impassioned brushwork
