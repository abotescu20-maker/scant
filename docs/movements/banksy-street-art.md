# Banksy / Street Art

**Period:** c. 1990s-present (emerged from Bristol UK graffiti scene)

## Overview

A contemporary form of public art that grew out of graffiti, hip-hop culture, and punk agitprop. Banksy (pseudonymous, active from the early 1990s) became its best-known practitioner, but the movement includes stencil, mural, paste-up and installation work placed illicitly in public space. Content is typically satirical, anti-war, anti-authoritarian, or critical of consumer capitalism.

## Technique

- **Stencil cut** — image cut from card or acetate; enables fast, repeatable application in public under time pressure
- **Spray paint** (aerosol), often multi-layer stencils for shading
- Frequent use of **wheatpaste** posters for larger works
- Site-specific placement: the wall, door, or surrounding architecture is integral to the joke
- Monochrome silhouettes with a single spot colour (red balloon, pink flowers) as visual punctuation

## Visual signatures for AI

- Flat, high-contrast stencil silhouettes
- Textured brick, concrete, or peeling-poster backgrounds
- One saturated accent colour against black-and-white figure
- Ironic juxtaposition: child + military, policeman + flowers, surveillance camera + kiss
- Drips and overspray at stencil edges — not clean graphics
- Typography limited to stencilled block letters or spray-painted tags
