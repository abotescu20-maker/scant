# Surrealism

**Period:** 1924-1940s

## Style

- André Breton's 1924 manifesto — put Freudian dream and the unconscious on canvas
- two branches: illusionistic veristic (Dalí, Magritte, Tanguy) and automatist abstract (Miró, Masson, Ernst frottage)
- juxtaposition of the impossible in a precise real space
- the unconscious, desire, madness, the absurd

## Key artists

- [[dali|Dalí]]
- [[magritte|Magritte]]
- Max Ernst
- Miró
- Tanguy
- de Chirico (metaphysical precursor)
- Giacometti (sculpture)

## Techniques

- hyperreal academic brushwork applied to impossible dream content (Dalí — melting watches, elongated shadows)
- deadpan Belgian-realist style with displaced objects (Magritte — pipe that isn't a pipe, apple filling a room)
- frottage — rubbing pencil over textured surface to start an image (Ernst)
- decalcomania — pressing wet paint between surfaces to create suggestive textures
- automatic drawing — letting the hand move without conscious control
- empty de Chirico piazzas with long shadows and classical statues — the 'metaphysical' mood
