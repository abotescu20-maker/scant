# Flemish Baroque

**Period:** 1600-1680

## Style

- exuberant sensual abundance — fleshy bodies, swirling silks, piles of fruit
- Catholic (Counter-Reformation) in the southern Netherlands
- warm ruddy skin tones painted with translucent glazes over red underlayer
- sweeping diagonal movement
- allegorical and mythological grandeur for courts and altars
- large workshop production — pupils blocked in, master finished heads and hands

## Key artists

- [[peter-paul-rubens|Peter Paul Rubens]]
- Anthony van Dyck
- Jacob Jordaens
- Frans Snyders

## Techniques

- translucent glazes over warm ground for luminous flesh
- broad wet-in-wet brushwork on the highlights
- 'coloured shadow' — cool greenish/grey-blue in shadow against warm lit flesh
- bozzetto oil sketch as compositional blueprint
- loose calligraphic drawing within resolved form
