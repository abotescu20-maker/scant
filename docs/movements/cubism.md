# Cubism

**Period:** 1907-1920

## Style

- objects broken into geometric facets and reassembled
- multiple viewpoints shown simultaneously on the same flat surface
- rejection of single-vanishing-point perspective after 500 years
- Analytic Cubism (1909-1912): monochrome brown/grey, object almost dissolved into grid of planes
- Synthetic Cubism (1912-1914): brighter colour, collage, clearer shapes built up rather than broken down
- Picasso's 'Les Demoiselles d'Avignon' 1907 as ignition — African mask influence

## Key artists

- [[picasso|Picasso]]
- [[braque|Braque]]
- Juan Gris
- Léger
- Delaunay (Orphic offshoot)

## Techniques

- faceting: objects split into flat planes as if seen from several angles at once
- shallow, almost relief-like picture space; no atmospheric depth
- dun palette of ochre, grey, brown in Analytic phase
- papier collé / collage — pasted newspaper, wallpaper, oilcloth, cork
- stencilled letters and musical symbols (WINE, JOURNAL, treble clef)
- trompe-l'œil faux-bois (wood-graining) combs dragged across paint
- sand, sawdust mixed into paint for physical texture
