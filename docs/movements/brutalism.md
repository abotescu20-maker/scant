# Brutalism

**Period:** c. 1951-1975 (revival from 2010s)

## Overview

Brutalism is a post-war architectural movement characterised by raw, board-formed concrete, monumental massing, and honest expression of structure and services. The name derives from *béton brut* (French: "raw concrete"), a term used by [[le-corbusier|Le Corbusier]] to describe the exposed concrete of the Unité d'Habitation in Marseille (1947-52). The movement was codified in Britain by Alison and Peter Smithson, and critic Reyner Banham's 1955 essay "The New Brutalism" in the *Architectural Review*, later expanded into a 1966 book.

## Technique

- cast-in-place reinforced concrete with visible timber-plank shuttering patterns
- deliberate exposure of structural systems, mechanical ducts, and service cores
- heavy, blocky, sculptural massing often cantilevered over voids
- modular repetition of cell-like units (housing, dorms, offices)
- minimal applied finishes: concrete, brick, glass, exposed steel
- honest expression of function (Smithsons' "as found" philosophy)

## Key practitioners and works

- [[le-corbusier|Le Corbusier]] — Unité d'Habitation (1952), Chandigarh Capitol Complex (1951-65)
- Alison and Peter Smithson — Robin Hood Gardens, London (1972, demolished 2017)
- [[ernoe-goldfinger|Ernő Goldfinger]] — Trellick Tower, London (1972)
- [[marcel-breuer|Marcel Breuer]] — Whitney Museum (1966), UNESCO HQ Paris (1958)
- Paul Rudolph — Yale Art & Architecture Building (1963)
- Louis Kahn — Salk Institute (1965)
- Kenzō Tange — Yamanashi Press Centre (1966)

## Visual signatures for AI

- [[board-formed-concrete|board-formed concrete]] surface with plank-grain imprint
- deep recessed windows creating strong shadow patterns
- [[monumental-scale|monumental scale]] — human figures dwarfed by slab forms
- [[raw-material-aesthetic|raw material aesthetic]] with no ornament
- pilotis (elevated columns), brise-soleil (sun breakers), ribbon glazing
- orthogonal geometry, occasionally softened by curves (Ronchamp)
- overcast northern European light; sometimes harsh tropical sun in Chandigarh
