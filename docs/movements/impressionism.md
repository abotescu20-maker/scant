# Impressionism

**Period:** c. 1860-1886

## Style

- painting the fleeting optical sensation, not the 'known' thing
- outdoor (en plein air) painting of modern life and landscape
- broken colour: small unblended strokes that mix in the viewer's eye
- shadows are coloured, never black
- the subject is really light itself — same haystack / cathedral at different hours

## Techniques

- short, comma-like broken brushstrokes (touches)
- juxtaposed complementary colours (orange next to blue) vibrating optically
- white ground instead of dark ground — whole canvas is high-key
- flat, silhouette-like figures influenced by Japanese woodblock prints
- cropped, off-centre compositions borrowed from photography and ukiyo-e (Degas)
- no blended modelling — form is built of coloured patches of light
