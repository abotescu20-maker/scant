# Neoclassicism

**Period:** 1760-1830

## Style

- reaction against Rococo frivolity — return to Greek/Roman gravitas
- inspired by excavations at Herculaneum and Pompeii
- clear linear contour, smooth enamel-like finish, no visible brushwork
- friezelike planar composition, figures arranged parallel to picture plane
- cool classicizing palette: stone, ivory, terracotta, crimson accent
- moral/civic subjects — oath, sacrifice, stoicism (republican virtue)
- severe architectural backgrounds (columns, arches, pared geometry)

## Techniques

- smooth licked finish, brushwork invisible
- primacy of drawing (disegno) over colour
- sculptural modelling of drapery with sharp folds
- blanched marble sculpture polished to flesh-like sheen
- idealized 'perfect' contour, line over tone
- archaeologically accurate props and costume
- crisp contour drawing, low-relief composition
- shallow stage-like space with figures parallel to picture plane
