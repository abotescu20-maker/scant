# Cyberpunk

**Period:** c. 1982-present (golden age 1982-1999)

## Overview

Cyberpunk is a visual and narrative genre that fuses high technology with social decay ("high tech, low life"). The look crystallised with Ridley Scott's *Blade Runner* (1982), whose production designs by [[syd-mead|Syd Mead]] and art director Lawrence G. Paull established the canonical palette: rain-soaked megacities, towering billboards, neon-drenched streets, perpetual night. The aesthetic draws from 1970s-80s Tokyo, Hong Kong's Kowloon Walled City, American noir cinema, and Japanese manga (Katsuhiro Otomo's *Akira*, 1988; Masamune Shirow's *Ghost in the Shell*, 1989).

## Technique

- dense layering of neon signage, kanji/katakana typography, and corporate logos
- volumetric atmosphere: rain, fog, steam vents, particulate haze
- chromatic aberration, lens flares, anamorphic distortion from filmic lenses
- high-contrast low-key lighting with saturated accent colours (cyan, magenta, sodium orange)
- practical miniatures (*Blade Runner*) later merged with CGI (*The Matrix*, 1999)
- matte painting of vertical-stacked megastructures influenced by [[brutalism|Brutalism]] and Hugh Ferriss's 1929 *Metropolis of Tomorrow*

## Key practitioners and works

- [[syd-mead|Syd Mead]] — concept artist (*Blade Runner*, *Tron*, *Aliens*)
- [[ridley-scott|Ridley Scott]] — director, *Blade Runner* (1982)
- [[mobius|Jean "Mœbius" Giraud]] — *Heavy Metal*, *The Long Tomorrow* (1976, with Dan O'Bannon) — prefigured the look
- Katsuhiro Otomo — *Akira* (manga 1982-90, anime 1988)
- Mamoru Oshii — *Ghost in the Shell* (1995)
- the Wachowskis — *The Matrix* (1999)

- flying vehicles, retrofitted analogue tech, cables and tubing
- extreme vertical compositions: skyscrapers, ad blimps, crowded street level
