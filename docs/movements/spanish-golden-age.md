# Spanish Golden Age

**Period:** 1600-1680

## Style

- austere Catholic mysticism meeting court realism
- restrained silver-grey-black palette on red-brown ground
- psychological portraiture — sitters scrutinized without flattery
- dwarfs, servants, and kings rendered with equal dignity
- bodegón (kitchen/tavern still-life) as serious subject

## Key artists

- [[diego-velazquez|Diego Velázquez]]
- [[el-greco|El Greco]]
- Francisco de Zurbarán
- Bartolomé Esteban Murillo
- Jusepe de Ribera

## Techniques

- loose 'touches' that resolve at distance (anticipating Impressionism)
- silvery atmospheric greys for court portraits
- deep velvety blacks next to luminous whites
- compositional mirrors (Las Meninas) and self-reflexive viewpoints
- tenebrist cell-like interiors for monks and saints
