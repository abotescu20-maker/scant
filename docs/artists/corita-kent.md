# Corita Kent

**Dates:** 1918–1986
**Movement:** serigraph / precursor to [[risograph|Risograph]] aesthetic

## Signature style

- American artist and Roman Catholic nun (Sister Mary Corita), head of the art department at Immaculate Heart College, Los Angeles, through the 1960s
- produced hundreds of silkscreen prints that combined advertising slogans, newspaper fragments, song lyrics and scripture in bright flat spot colours
- her prints pre-figure the Risograph aesthetic: limited fluorescent/spot palettes, exuberant hand-cut type, deliberate layering of semi-transparent inks, deliberate imperfection and roughness
- civil-rights and anti-war engagement: 1985 "LOVE" stamp for the US Postal Service, the *Rainbow Swash* gas tank in Boston (1971)

## Visual signatures for AI

- flat saturated spot colours (pink, red, yellow, blue) layered on white or cream
- hand-cut or hand-torn letterforms, wobbling baselines
- quoted slogans ("come alive", "wonderbread") collaged over painterly grounds
- overprint patches where two inks cross
- grainy screen-texture in solids, occasional misregistration
