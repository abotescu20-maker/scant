# Mœbius (Jean Giraud)

**Dates:** 1938-2012
**Movement:** [[ligne-claire|Ligne claire]], [[cyberpunk|Cyberpunk]] (proto-)

## Signature style

- French comics master who signed Western comics as "Gir" (*Blueberry*, 1963-) and sci-fi as "Mœbius" (from 1963, prominent from 1975)
- co-founded *Métal Hurlant* (1975), the magazine that reshaped adult sci-fi comics
- razor-fine ink line with dense hatching for texture, but flat-colour clarity at distance
- endless desert vistas, mutating biomechanical forms, floating cities
- *The Long Tomorrow* (1976, scripted by Dan O'Bannon) directly inspired *Blade Runner* and *Alien* production design

## Visual contribution

- bridged European [[ligne-claire|ligne claire]] tradition with American pulp sci-fi
- conceptual designer for *Alien* (1979), *Tron* (1982), *The Fifth Element* (1997), *The Abyss* (1989)
- influenced a generation: Miyazaki, Otomo, Jodorowsky (*The Incal*, 1980-88)
- used clear-line contour even inside baroque sci-fi scenes — legibility preserved
