# Joost Swarte

**Dates:** 1947- (Dutch)
**Movement:** [[ligne-claire|Ligne claire]]

## Signature style

- Dutch cartoonist, illustrator, graphic and architectural designer
- coined the phrase "klare lijn" ("clear line") in 1977 for an exhibition on Hergé's tradition at Museum Boijmans Van Beuningen, Rotterdam
- minimalist variant: tighter, more geometric, often with Art Deco references
- covers for *The New Yorker* (regular contributor since 2000s)
- designed Toneelschuur theatre, Haarlem (2003) applying ligne claire logic to architecture
- also designs postage stamps, typefaces, furniture

## Visual contribution

- theorist as well as practitioner — gave the school its name
- pushed ligne claire toward graphic-design purity rather than adventure narrative
- architectural work demonstrates the style's compositional reach beyond comics
