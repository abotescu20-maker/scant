# Apelles

**Dates:** c.370-300 BC
**Movement:** Late Classical Greek

## Signature style

- Legendary court painter to Alexander the Great whose works are all lost; known through ancient writers who praised his charis (grace), incomparable flesh tones, atmospheric depth, and four-pigment palette of red, yellow, black and white.

## Key works

- Alexander Wielding Thunderbolt (lost)
- Venus Anadyomene (lost)
- Calumny (lost, reconstructed by Botticelli)

## Lineage

**Influenced by:** Pamphilos of Sicyon
**Influenced:** Roman painters, Renaissance idea of the supreme painter (emulated by Raphael and Botticelli)
