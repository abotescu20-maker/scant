# Praxiteles

**Dates:** c.395-330 BC
**Movement:** Late Classical Greek

## Signature style

- Softened classical ideal into sensuous living flesh. Introduced the relaxed S-curve stance and the female nude (Aphrodite of Knidos). Surfaces polished to translucence so marble seems to breathe; gazes are dreamy, half-turned; drapery slips suggestively.

## Key works

- Hermes with Infant Dionysus
- Aphrodite of Knidos (lost)
- Apollo Sauroktonos

## Lineage

**Influenced by:** Phidias, Polykleitos
**Influenced:** Hellenistic sensuality, Roman copyists, Canova, 19th-c academic nudes
