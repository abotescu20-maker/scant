# Daswanth

**c.1550 - 1584, Mughal imperial atelier**

## Overview

Daswanth (also Dasavanta) was a Hindu painter of low-born origin whom Emperor Akbar personally elevated into the imperial *tasvir khana*. Abu'l-Fazl, in the *Ain-i-Akbari*, called him a marvel of his age and claimed he surpassed all painters of the world. He was a principal illustrator of the *Razmnama* — Akbar's commissioned Persian translation of the Sanskrit *Mahabharata* — before his early death by suicide in 1584.

## Contribution

- bold, inventive figural energy within dense battle and court scenes
- rich colour dynamic that balanced Persian elegance with Indian saturation
- early example of an Indian-born painter leading narrative illustration at the Mughal court
- key contributor to the first Mughal *Razmnama* (1582-86, Jaipur City Palace)

## Key works

- *Razmnama* (Jaipur City Palace Museum, 1582-86) — numerous folios
- *Tutinama* illustrations (Cleveland Museum of Art)
- *Akbarnama* contributions
