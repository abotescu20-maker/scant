# Mathew Brady

**Dates:** c. 1822–1896
**Movement:** [[daguerreotype|Daguerreotype]]

## Signature style

- most famous early American daguerreotypist; ran portrait "galleries" on Broadway, New York, and on Pennsylvania Avenue, Washington, D.C.
- systematically photographed public figures — presidents, generals, writers — building the first large visual archive of American political life
- organised and largely financed photographic coverage of the American Civil War (1861–1865), much of it executed by his employees Alexander Gardner and Timothy O'Sullivan using the later wet-plate collodion process rather than daguerreotypes
- brought daguerreotype portraiture to industrial scale: standing backdrops, plain sitter chairs with head-clamps, controlled north-light studio

## Visual signatures for AI

- cased silver-plate portraits: bust or three-quarter length, sitter seated, hand often resting on a book or cane
- plain neutral painted backdrop, single strong diagonal north light
- immobile expression, steady direct gaze, formal dress
- later Civil War work shifts to albumen prints and *cartes de visite*, but visual signature stays restrained, documentary, frontal
