# Reza Abbasi

**c.1565 - 1635, Isfahan**

## Overview

Reza Abbasi is the leading painter of the Isfahan school under Shah Abbas I, who awarded him the honorific "Abbasi." He shifted Persian miniature practice away from dense illustrated manuscripts toward the single-page album drawing — a sheet showing one or two figures, a calligraphic cartouche, and a delicately tinted ground. His fluid line and observation of contemporary Isfahan life (dervishes, youths, European visitors) made him the signature artist of [[persian-miniature|late Safavid art]].

## Contribution

- single-figure album compositions that defined late Safavid taste
- calligraphic, varying-weight line as principal expressive device
- looser colour and exposed paper, contrasting with Timurid density
- subjects drawn from everyday Isfahan — not only courtly romance

## Key works

- *Youth Reading* (1626, Metropolitan Museum of Art, 1975.192.4)
- *Two Lovers* (c.1630, Met, 50.164)
- calligraphic album pages signed *"ya sahib al-zaman"*
