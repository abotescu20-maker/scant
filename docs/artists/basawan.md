# Basawan

**Active c.1580 - 1600, Mughal imperial atelier**

## Overview

Basawan (also Basavan) was a Hindu painter in the Mughal imperial *tasvir khana* under Emperor Akbar and one of the artists most praised in Abu'l-Fazl's *Ain-i-Akbari*. He is celebrated as the painter who most fully absorbed European naturalism — arriving through Jesuit prints and oil paintings — into the composite [[mughal-miniature|Mughal manner]]. Akbar's chronicler ranked him among the first painters of the age.

## Contribution

- psychological portraiture with modelled cheeks, brows, and gaze direction
- depth through overlapping figure groups and receding landscape
- inventive narrative compositions for the *Akbarnama* and *Razmnama*
- training his son Manohar, later a major Jahangiri portraitist

## Key works

- *Akbarnama* folios (c.1590-95, Victoria and Albert Museum)
- *Razmnama* illustrations (Persian *Mahabharata*, 1582-86)
- "Adham Khan pays homage to Akbar" (V&A IS.2:21-1896)
