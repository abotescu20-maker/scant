# Kazuo Oga

**Born 1952, Akita Prefecture, Japan**

## Overview

Kazuo Oga is a Japanese background artist and art director whose watercolour paintings define the visual world of many [[ghibli-studio|Studio Ghibli]] films. Self-taught as a painter, he joined Ghibli as art director for *My Neighbor Totoro* (1988) and then continued on *Only Yesterday*, *Pom Poko*, *Princess Mononoke*, *Spirited Away*, and *The Boy and the Heron*. Where Miyazaki drew the characters and their movement, Oga painted the worlds in which they moved: mossy forest floors, moist rice paddies, empty school corridors in summer.

## Contribution

- [[watercolour-wash-backgrounds|transparent watercolour wash]] as principal background medium, over lightly gouached base
- non-outlined foliage that shimmers rather than diagrams the leaf
- observational realism of Japanese countryside — specific tree species, moss, wet earth, rust
- training a generation of Ghibli background artists in his method

## Key works

- background art direction, *My Neighbor Totoro* (1988)
- background art direction, *Only Yesterday* (1991), *Pom Poko* (1994)
- background art, *Princess Mononoke* (1997), *Spirited Away* (2001), *The Boy and the Heron* (2023)
