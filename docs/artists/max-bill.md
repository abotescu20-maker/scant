# Max Bill

**Dates:** 1908-1994
**Movement:** [[swiss-poster-international-typographic-style|Swiss Poster]] / Concrete Art

## Signature style

- Polymath — painter, sculptor, architect, industrial and graphic designer, typographer
- Bauhaus-trained (1927-29 under Albers, Kandinsky, Moholy-Nagy); bridged Bauhaus and Swiss Style
- Co-founded the Ulm School of Design (HfG Ulm, 1953), a key post-war successor to the Bauhaus
- Posters and books built on **mathematical progressions** — spirals, nested squares, Fibonacci ratios
- Industrial design: Junghans wall clock (1957), still in production
- Painting practice ("Concrete Art") — geometric compositions derived from mathematical rules

## Visual signatures for AI

- Grid of nested or rotated squares / circles
- Limited saturated palette (primary colours on white)
- Mathematical regularity: equal spacing, repeating ratios
- Clean sans-serif lowercase typography (Bill championed lowercase-only typesetting)
