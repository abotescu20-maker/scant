# Kamal ud-Din Behzad

**c.1455 - c.1535, Herat and Tabriz**

## Overview

Kamal ud-Din Behzad (also Bihzad) is the central master of the Timurid Herat school and a founding influence on [[persian-miniature|Safavid Persian miniature]] painting. Trained under Mirak Naqqash in the ketab-khaneh of Sultan Husayn Bayqara, he was later head of the royal library of Shah Isma'il I in Tabriz. Contemporaries regarded him as a reviver of the art; Babur and later Mughal patrons collected his work, and his students transmitted his manner into the imperial [[mughal-miniature|Mughal studio]].

## Contribution

- introduction of individualised facial types and naturalistic gesture into a traditionally hieratic idiom
- more complex spatial arrangement with multiple figure groups staged on a tilted ground plane
- sensitive observation of labourers, builders, and villagers alongside courtly scenes
- refined integration of architectural ornament, text, and figure

## Key works

- *Bustan of Sa'di* (1488, now Cairo, National Library of Egypt)
- *Khamsa of Nizami* illustrations (1494-95, British Library Or.6810)
- *Zafarnama of Timur* pages (Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore)
