# Hergé (Georges Remi)

**Dates:** 1907-1983 (Belgian)
**Movement:** [[ligne-claire|Ligne claire]]

## Signature style

- pen name from his initials reversed: R.G. → "Hergé"
- created Tintin in *Le Petit Vingtième* (10 January 1929) — *Tintin au pays des Soviets*
- 24 albums across his lifetime, last unfinished *Tintin et l'Alph-Art* (1986 posthumous)
- developed the ligne claire aesthetic: uniform contour, flat colour, no hatching
- documentary-precision research for settings and costumes (notably *The Blue Lotus*, 1934-35, with Zhang Chongren)
- circular-headed characters with minimal facial geometry — eyes as dots or dashes
- after WWII founded Studios Hergé (1950) with Bob de Moor, Jacques Martin, Edgar P. Jacobs

## Visual contribution

- every panel legible as a silhouette — no element lost in rendering
- regulated a six-tier page grid adopted across Franco-Belgian comics
- coined no theoretical term himself; [[joost-swarte|Joost Swarte]] named it "ligne claire" in 1977
