# Giotto di Bondone

**Dates:** c.1267-1337
**Movement:** Italian Proto-Renaissance (Trecento)

## Signature style

- Broke from flat Byzantine pattern by modelling figures as heavy three-dimensional bodies lit by directional light, grouped in simple stage-like space. Faces show interior feeling - grief, tenderness, rage - through posture and gesture. Colors are sober earth tones on blue ground; drapery falls in weighted folds you could grab.

## Key works

- Arena Chapel frescoes, Padua (1305-06)
- The Mourning of Christ (Lamentation)
- St Francis cycle at Assisi (attributed)
- Ognissanti Madonna

## Lineage

**Influenced by:** Cimabue, Roman late-antique painting, Nicola Pisano's classicizing relief
**Influenced:** Masaccio, all of Italian Renaissance painting, Michelangelo's figure style
