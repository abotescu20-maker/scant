# Sultan Mohammed

**Active c.1515 - c.1560, Tabriz**

## Overview

Sultan Mohammed was the foremost painter of the early Safavid court at Tabriz under Shah Isma'il I and Shah Tahmasp I, and effective director of the royal atelier that produced the *Shahnameh* of Shah Tahmasp (often called the *Houghton Shahnameh*, c.1525-40). He synthesised the refinement of the Herat school of [[behzad|Behzad]] with the energy of the older Turkman tradition, creating the grand ceremonial manner of mature [[persian-miniature|Persian miniature]].

## Contribution

- ambitious multi-plane compositions filled with animated figures
- cloud-swirling gold skies and rocky landscapes populated with hidden faces
- integration of Qizilbash court costume into the epic world of the *Shahnameh*
- training of the generation that would dominate mid-16th-century Persian painting

## Key works

- *The Court of Gayumars* (c.1525-30, Aga Khan Museum) — often considered the single greatest Persian miniature
- *Shahnameh* of Shah Tahmasp illustrations (dispersed; major holdings at the Met, Aga Khan Museum)
