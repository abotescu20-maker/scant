# Le Corbusier

**Dates:** 1887-1965 (born Charles-Édouard Jeanneret-Gris, Switzerland)
**Movement:** Modernism, [[brutalism|Brutalism]]

## Signature style

- coined the term *béton brut* ("raw concrete") that gave [[brutalism|Brutalism]] its name
- five points of architecture (1926): pilotis, free plan, free façade, ribbon windows, roof garden
- Modulor proportional system (1948) based on a 1.83m human figure
- early white Purist villas (Villa Savoye, 1931) shifted post-war to heavy concrete
- Unité d'Habitation, Marseille (1947-52) — the prototype Brutalist residential slab
- Chandigarh Capitol Complex, India (1951-65) — monumental concrete civic centre
- Notre-Dame-du-Haut, Ronchamp (1955) — sculptural curved concrete chapel

## Visual contribution

- exposed shuttering marks of timber formwork became a deliberate aesthetic
- combined sculptural massing with rigorous geometric proportion
- his post-war work directly inspired the Smithsons, Rudolph, Breuer, Kahn
