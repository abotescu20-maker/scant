# Phidias

**Dates:** c.490-430 BC
**Movement:** Classical Greek (High Classical)

## Signature style

- Master of idealized divine calm. Modeled colossal chryselephantine statues (gold and ivory on wooden core) of Athena Parthenos and Zeus at Olympia, and supervised the Parthenon marbles. Drapery flows in wet-cloth patterns revealing body beneath; faces radiate serene impassive dignity; proportions embody Periclean balance.

## Key works

- Parthenon sculptures (Athens)
- Athena Parthenos (lost)
- Zeus at Olympia (lost)
- Parthenon frieze

## Lineage

**Influenced by:** archaic kouros tradition, Polygnotos
**Influenced:** all subsequent classical sculpture, Michelangelo, Neoclassicism
