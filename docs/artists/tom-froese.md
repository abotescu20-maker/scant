# Tom Froese

**Dates:** b. 1977, Canada
**Movement:** [[risograph|Risograph]]

## Signature style

- Canadian illustrator and educator, widely associated with the contemporary Risograph / flat-colour digital illustration aesthetic
- editorial, packaging and children's-book illustrator whose work circulates both as digital CMYK and as limited-run Riso prints
- his design vocabulary — flat geometric shapes, warm off-white backgrounds, deliberate two-to-four spot-colour palettes, intentional "misregistration" overlays — helped popularise the Riso look among illustrators who do not own a Riso machine
- teaches online courses (Skillshare, Domestika) that explicitly introduce thousands of illustrators to Risograph-inspired workflows

## Visual signatures for AI

- 2–4 flat spot colours (coral / warm pink, federal blue, yellow, mint) on cream paper
- chunky rounded geometric figures and still-life objects
- visible coarse halftone textures inside solid shapes
- slight offset of one colour layer against another (digital mimic of Riso misregistration)
- thick uneven outlines, hand-feeling though digitally drawn
