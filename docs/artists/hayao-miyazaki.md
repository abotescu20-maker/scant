# Hayao Miyazaki

**Born 1941, Tokyo**

## Overview

Hayao Miyazaki is a Japanese animator, manga artist, and co-founder (1985) of [[ghibli-studio|Studio Ghibli]] with Isao Takahata and Toshio Suzuki. Beginning at Toei Animation in 1963, he directed *Nausicaa of the Valley of the Wind* (1984) before launching Ghibli. His films combine hand-drawn key animation, detailed watercolour environments, pacifist and ecological themes, and young (often female) protagonists navigating liminal worlds. He won the Academy Award for *Spirited Away* (2002) and a second for *The Boy and the Heron* (2024).

## Contribution

- insistence on hand-drawn animation even as the industry moved to CGI
- "ma" — the held empty moment — as structural device
- flying sequences as core emotional register, drawn from his father's aviation factory background
- environmental and anti-war themes without didactic voice-over

## Key works

- *My Neighbor Totoro* (1988)
- *Kiki's Delivery Service* (1989)
- *Princess Mononoke* (1997)
- *Spirited Away* (2001) — Academy Award, Golden Bear
- *Howl's Moving Castle* (2004)
- *The Wind Rises* (2013)
- *The Boy and the Heron* (2023) — Academy Award
