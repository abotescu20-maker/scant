# Ridley Scott

**Dates:** 1937-
**Movement:** [[cyberpunk|Cyberpunk]] (as visual director)

## Signature style

- trained at the Royal College of Art as a designer before directing
- background in British television advertising shaped his painterly frame composition
- directed *Blade Runner* (1982), the film that codified cyberpunk visual language
- earlier *Alien* (1979) established his industrial-decay aesthetic
- characteristic use of volumetric lighting: backlit smoke, rain, shafts from practical sources
- storyboarded every shot personally ("Ridleygrams")

## Visual contribution

- hired [[syd-mead|Syd Mead]] as futurist; art-directed a nocturnal, rain-sodden Los Angeles
- combined film noir chiaroscuro with Asian market-street density
- every surface in *Blade Runner* wet, reflective, back-lit — became genre default
- later *Gladiator* (2000) re-applied the same atmospheric layering to ancient Rome
