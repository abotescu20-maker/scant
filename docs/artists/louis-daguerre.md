# Louis Daguerre

**Dates:** 1787–1851
**Movement:** [[daguerreotype|Daguerreotype]]

## Signature style

- invented the daguerreotype, the first publicly available photographic process, announced 19 August 1839 at a joint session of the Académie des Sciences and the Académie des Beaux-Arts in Paris
- earlier career as a scene-painter and co-inventor of the Diorama (1822), a theatre of large painted translucent scenes lit from in front and behind
- his surviving daguerreotypes include a Paris boulevard view (*Boulevard du Temple*, 1838) in which a man having his boots polished is the first known human being photographed — the rest of the street, moving too fast for the long exposure, disappears
- the French government purchased the patent in 1839 and "gave it to the world", accelerating its adoption everywhere except Britain, where Daguerre kept the patent

## Visual signatures for AI

- silver-plate, mirror surface, cased presentation
- frozen long-exposure street — empty pavements, single still figures
- classical pictorial framing carried over from his diorama backdrops
- cool neutral silver tone, sometimes very lightly hand-tinted
