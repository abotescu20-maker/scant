# Marcel Breuer

**Dates:** 1902-1981 (Hungarian-American)
**Movement:** [[brutalism|Brutalism]], Bauhaus, Modernism

## Signature style

- Bauhaus-trained (Dessau, 1920s) where he designed the Wassily chair (1925) and Cesca chair (1928)
- emigrated via London to Harvard (1937), taught alongside Gropius
- shifted in the 1950s from steel-tube modernism to heavy sculptural concrete
- Whitney Museum of American Art, New York (1966) — inverted ziggurat of granite-clad concrete
- UNESCO Headquarters, Paris (1958) — Y-shaped block with Pier Luigi Nervi
- Saint John's Abbey Church, Minnesota (1961) — concrete bell banner, trapezoidal windows
- Pirelli Tire Building, New Haven (1970) — concrete sunshades

## Visual contribution

- combined Bauhaus geometric rigour with Brutalist mass
- pioneered precast concrete panels with sculptural three-dimensional profiles
- deep-set trapezoidal windows created dramatic shadow geometry on façades
