# Khwaja Abd al-Samad

**c.1505 - c.1600, Shiraz and Mughal imperial atelier**

## Overview

Khwaja Abd al-Samad was a Persian master from Shiraz invited by Emperor Humayun to India around 1550 and retained by Akbar as joint head — with Mir Sayyid Ali — of the imperial painting workshop. He is a hinge figure between [[persian-miniature|Persian miniature]] and the emerging [[mughal-miniature|Mughal idiom]]: his refined Safavid training became the technical backbone that Indian artists like [[basawan|Basawan]] and [[daswanth|Daswanth]] then extended toward naturalism.

## Contribution

- codification of the Mughal studio method — team production with *tarh* (outline), *rangamizi* (colouring), *chihranama* (face)
- transmission of Safavid line and [[gold-leaf-illumination|gold illumination]] to Indian painters
- supervision of the *Hamzanama*, the first great Mughal illustrated cycle (c.1558-73, ~1,400 folios)
- tutor to the young Akbar in drawing

## Key works

- *Hamzanama* folios (Museum of Applied Arts, Vienna; V&A; Brooklyn Museum)
- *Princes of the House of Timur* (with Mir Sayyid Ali, British Museum)
- small-scale signed album pages
