# Isao Takahata

**1935 - 2018, Tokyo**

## Overview

Isao Takahata was a Japanese director and co-founder (1985) of [[ghibli-studio|Studio Ghibli]] with Hayao Miyazaki and Toshio Suzuki. Unlike Miyazaki, he was not a draughtsman; his contribution was directorial — structuring narrative, pushing formal experiment, and coaxing new visual registers out of his art departments. His films range from neorealist wartime tragedy (*Grave of the Fireflies*) to brush-and-watercolour pastoral (*The Tale of the Princess Kaguya*), all sharing a documentary-like attention to gesture, food, labour, and the textures of rural Japan.

## Contribution

- repeated formal reinvention rather than a single visual brand
- pioneering digital compositing in *My Neighbors the Yamadas* (1999)
- sketch-on-paper aesthetic of *Princess Kaguya* (2013) with exposed linework and unfinished margins
- dignified treatment of childhood, grief, and working life

## Key works

- *Grave of the Fireflies* (1988)
- *Only Yesterday* (1991)
- *Pom Poko* (1994)
- *My Neighbors the Yamadas* (1999)
- *The Tale of the Princess Kaguya* (2013) — Academy Award nominee
