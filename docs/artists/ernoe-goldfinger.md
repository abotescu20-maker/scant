# Ernő Goldfinger

**Dates:** 1902-1987 (Hungarian-British)
**Movement:** [[brutalism|Brutalism]], Modernism

## Signature style

- Budapest-born, Paris-trained under Auguste Perret, practised in London from 1934
- master of urban concrete residential towers for London County Council
- Trellick Tower, North Kensington (1972) — 31-storey slab with detached service tower linked by bridges
- Balfron Tower, Poplar (1967) — earlier prototype of the same typology
- 2 Willow Road, Hampstead (1939) — his own modernist house, now National Trust
- offices at Alexander Fleming House, Elephant & Castle (1963, demolished in part)

## Visual contribution

- separated services (lifts, refuse chutes) into a distinct slender tower — became a Brutalist trope
- rough board-marked concrete with strong horizontal balcony shadow lines
- composed towers as visible sculptural hierarchies rather than uniform slabs
- Ian Fleming reportedly named the Bond villain Goldfinger in protest at his modernism
