# Stencil cut

## Technique

An image is cut out of a thin, rigid material (card, acetate, mylar) as negative space. The stencil is held against a surface and a single flat colour — usually spray paint — is applied through the openings. Multi-layer stencils separate tonal values or colours into a registered stack of sheets, producing halftone-like shading.

Characteristic features:

- Hard, crisp edges wherever the stencil seals against the wall
- Thin bridges of material must connect "islands" of the design (e.g. the inside of a letter O) — these bridges remain visible as small gaps in the final image
- Slight drip and overspray at edges where aerosol bleeds past the stencil
- Very fast to apply — makes it practical for illicit street work
