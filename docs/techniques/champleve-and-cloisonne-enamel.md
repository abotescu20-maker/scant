# champlevé and cloisonné enamel

## Technique

Champlevé: hollows carved out of a copper or bronze plate are filled with powdered coloured glass and fired so the glass fuses. Cloisonné: thin metal strips soldered to a base form cells (cloisons) that are filled with glass paste and fired. Produces jewel-bright small-scale decoration on reliquaries, book-covers and liturgical vessels.
