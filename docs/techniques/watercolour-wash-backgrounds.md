# Watercolour Wash Backgrounds

## Overview

The hand-painted watercolour background is the single most recognisable visual feature of [[ghibli-studio|Studio Ghibli]] films, codified by art director [[kazuo-oga|Kazuo Oga]] from *My Neighbor Totoro* (1988) onward. Where earlier TV anime relied on flat poster-colour backgrounds and heavy outlines, Oga's department used transparent watercolour washes on textured paper, preserving the grain of the paper and the pooling of pigment in the finished frame.

## Method

- pencil layout traced onto watercolour board
- lightly applied gouache or poster colour base for opaque zones (buildings, earth)
- transparent watercolour washes for foliage, sky, water, fabric in the landscape
- non-outlined foliage — the leaf is a colour shape, not a diagram
- salt, tissue, or wet-in-wet techniques for mossy texture
- scanned into digital compositing from *Princess Mononoke* (1997) onward, then married to [[cel-shading|cel-shaded]] characters

## Characteristics

- visible paper grain and wash edges
- softly varying greens within a single tree rather than flat fills
- cool, slightly hazy light rather than high contrast
- continuity of tone between foreground and distance

## Why it matters for AI

A Ghibli-style prompt that produces sharply outlined, vector-flat foliage misses the core aesthetic. The painterly drift of the wash and the visible paper are the style.
