# Fine Parallel Hatching (*Pardakht*)

## Overview

*Pardakht* is the characteristic Mughal method of modelling skin, fabric, and fur with extremely fine, closely spaced parallel strokes. It is the element that most clearly distinguishes [[mughal-miniature|Mughal miniature]] from its [[persian-miniature|Persian]] parent: where Persian painters preferred flat, unmodelled colour areas, Mughal painters from the Akbar period onward absorbed European engraving conventions and translated crosshatching into a gouache idiom.

## Method

- base colour laid as a flat wash
- sequential darker washes built up only in shadow zones
- fine brushwork in a slightly darker or warmer tone, parallel to the form
- often a second hatching layer perpendicular or diagonal for deepest shadows
- on faces, specialist *chihranama* painters produced cheek, nose, and chin modelling stroke by stroke

## Why it matters for AI

*Pardakht* is what gives Mughal faces their quiet three-dimensionality while the textiles and backgrounds behind them remain ornamentally flat. A generative model that either applies uniform soft shading everywhere, or leaves faces flat, misses this crucial tension between modelled figure and ornamental setting.
