# Clear line composition

## Technique

Page- and panel-level organisation in which every element sits in a legible place without overlap-ambiguity. A direct consequence of [[uniform-line-weight|uniform line weight]] and [[flat-color-fills|flat colour fills]]: if line and colour cannot convey hierarchy, composition must.

Principles:
- figures placed at the panel's orthogonal centres, not on edges
- one principal action per panel; secondary business kept peripheral
- cast-silhouettes don't merge — characters drawn apart, or with colour contrast when close
- backgrounds rendered in the same pictorial language as foregrounds (no blur, no desaturation)
- regular six- or eight-tier page grid, minimal inset or splash panels
- horizon lines consistent across a sequence to preserve spatial continuity
- documentary accuracy of vehicles, buildings, costumes — a reader should be able to identify period and place

## Practitioners

- [[herge|Hergé]] — canonical page-design grammar
- Edgar P. Jacobs — extends clear-line to dense technical settings (submarines, labs)
- Benoît Peeters and François Schuiten — *Les Cités obscures* (clear-line into imaginary architecture)
- [[joost-swarte|Joost Swarte]]
