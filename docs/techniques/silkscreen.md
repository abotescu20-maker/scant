# Silkscreen (serigraphy)

## Technique

A stencil-based printing process: ink is pushed with a squeegee through the open mesh areas of a fine fabric screen (originally silk, now polyester), while the closed areas block ink. Each colour requires a separate screen; prints are built up one layer at a time.

Characteristic features:

- Flat, opaque, saturated colour — no natural gradient within a pass
- Slight mis-registration between layers produces tell-tale colour offsets at edges
- Visible mesh or stipple texture inside large flat fields
- Well suited to cheap editions of hundreds to thousands of prints
- Adopted industrially in the 1930s for [[wpa-poster|WPA posters]] (Anthony Velonis championed it as fine art)
- Core medium of 1960s Pop Art (Warhol, Rauschenberg) and of [[banksy-street-art|Street Art]] editions
