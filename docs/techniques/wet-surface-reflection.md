# Wet surface reflection

## Technique

Rain-soaked asphalt, tile, and car bodywork used as a second visible sky or signage field. Every light source is doubled: once at origin, once as a vertically stretched, slightly blurred reflection in the ground plane. This:

- multiplies perceived neon count without adding more tubes
- introduces a secondary compositional axis below the horizon line
- separates figures visually — a silhouette on wet ground reads in two planes
- is maintained practically on set by continuous spraying between takes (*Blade Runner*)

Rendering rules:
- reflection is vertically elongated and soft-edged (surface tension scatter)
- contrast is reduced; saturation is preserved
- puddle breakage adds horizontal streaks of concentrated colour
- dry patches interrupt reflections and create compositional rhythm
