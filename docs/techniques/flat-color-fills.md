# Flat colour fills

## Technique

Enclosed contour regions filled with a single, unmodulated tone — no gradient, no hatching, no airbrush modelling, no cast shadow. Each colour is chosen from a limited palette and separated from its neighbours by the [[uniform-line-weight|uniform black contour line]]. Historically executed via printers' colour-separation overlays: the artist indicated fills on a tracing-paper layer keyed to the inked line, and the printer translated these to four-colour plates.

Visual consequences:
- complete legibility at any reproduction size
- every object looks "newly made" — no wear, no dirt, no weathering
- lighting is implied abstractly rather than depicted
- limited palette (8-16 base colours) forced careful compositional choices
- absence of shadow gives scenes a bright, even, timeless daylight
