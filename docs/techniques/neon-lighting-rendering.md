# Neon lighting rendering

## Technique

Representation of gas-discharge tube signage and LED-like point sources as dominant scene illumination. The saturated hue of each tube (classic neon orange-red, argon blue, mercury cyan-green) spills onto nearby surfaces as coloured rim-light and fill. In painting or digital rendering, this is faked by:

- painting the tube itself as a nearly-white hot core surrounded by a halo of its base hue
- applying a colour-dodge or screen-blend glow outward for 3-10× the tube diameter
- picking up bounce colour on any wet or reflective surface facing the sign
- using the complementary colour (often sodium orange) on shadow sides for chromatic contrast
- heavy atmospheric haze (rain, fog, smoke) to volumize the glow into visible light shafts
