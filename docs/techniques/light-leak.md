# Light Leak

## Technique

A light leak is any stray light reaching the film through a gap in the camera body — most often a worn or unsealed back door, a cracked bellows, or a loose lens mount. It exposes the film locally, producing vivid orange, red or yellow streaks across one edge of the frame or along the sprocket holes.

In precision photography light leaks are defects. In the [[lomography|Lomographic]] aesthetic they are a signature: cheap plastic cameras such as the Holga, Diana F+ and LC-A routinely leak, and users learned to exploit — or even encourage — the streaks with tape, rewind and deliberate back-cracking.

Visual markers:
- warm red / orange banding concentrated on one edge of the frame
- gradient falloff (bright at the leak, fading across the image)
- streaks crossing over the image itself, not behind it
- occasional piping of light along film perforations, producing a line of dots
- variation frame to frame, because the leak depends on how film is wound and how the camera is held
