# HUD overlay

## Technique

Heads-Up Display overlay — diegetic graphic layer composited on top of the photographic image, representing the point-of-view of a helmet, implant, vehicle cockpit, or surveillance camera. Visual elements include:

- targeting reticles, bracket tracking boxes on moving subjects
- scrolling text columns (telemetry, radar, biometric readouts)
- vector-style line graphics and simple geometric primitives
- monochromatic green or amber phosphor palette (early) or full colour (later)
- alignment constrained to rigorous grid or radial symmetry
- low opacity fill with high-opacity stroke

Historically hand-animated via photocopy and backlit cel (Apollo-era documentaries), then Scanimate, rotoscoping, and from the 1990s compositing in Flame/Nuke.

## Practitioners

- Ron Cobb, Dan O'Bannon — *Alien* (1979) "Mother" computer
- Syd Mead, John Scheele — *Blade Runner* Esper machine sequence
- Mark Coleran — *The Bourne Identity* (2002) and related espionage UI design
- Territory Studio — *Blade Runner 2049*, *The Martian*
