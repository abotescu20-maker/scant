# Cel Shading

## Overview

Cel shading is the traditional animation technique in which a drawn character is coloured in flat zones with one or two additional shadow tones — the shadow has a hard edge rather than a gradient. The name derives from the celluloid sheets (*cels*) on which the character was painted before being photographed over a painted background. [[ghibli-studio|Studio Ghibli]] preserved and refined this look even after its production pipeline became digital from *Princess Mononoke* (1997) onward.

## Method

- character key animation drawn on paper, cleaned up, and coloured
- each character painted in 2-3 tones per local colour: base, shadow, sometimes highlight
- shadow boundaries follow form but remain graphic, not blended
- character cels composited over [[watercolour-wash-backgrounds|watercolour backgrounds]]
- deliberate tonal tension between flat character and painterly environment

## Characteristics

- clean contour line around the character
- hard shadow terminators rather than soft gradients
- limited colour palette per character, consistent across shots
- the character reads as a drawn figure moving through a painted world

## Why it matters for AI

Generating a character with smooth photographic shading inside a Ghibli environment breaks the pipeline's foundational contrast: painterly background, drawn character. Cel shading is what keeps the character feeling hand-drawn.
