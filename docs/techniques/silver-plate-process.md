# Silver Plate Process

## Technique

The physical support of a daguerreotype: a thin sheet of copper electroplated or fused with a polished silver surface. The plate is buffed to a mirror finish, then sensitised by exposure to iodine (and later bromine) vapour, which converts silver atoms at the surface into light-sensitive silver halides. After exposure in the camera the plate is developed over mercury vapour, fixed in sodium thiosulphate, gilded with gold chloride to strengthen and tone the image, and sealed against air under glass, because atmospheric sulphur compounds tarnish silver within days.

The image sits *on* the silver surface, not in an emulsion layer above it: it is extraordinarily fine-grained and sharp, but also delicate, and it reverses from positive to negative depending on the angle of view.
