# Scan-lines

## Technique

Horizontal dark bands simulating the visible raster of an analogue CRT monitor or video tape artefact. A cultural signifier of "screen within the image" and of retrofuturist 1980s-90s technology. Typically applied as:

- a regular horizontal dark-line pattern at 50-60% of pixel height
- a subtle chromatic offset between R, G, B channels (chromatic aberration)
- occasional vertical rolling bar or sync glitch
- slight barrel distortion and vignette at corners (CRT curvature)
- bloom on bright pixels (phosphor overshoot)

In classical cel animation this was faked by drawing fine horizontal lines over photocopied interfaces; in digital compositing it is a standard shader.

## Practitioners

- Katsuhiro Otomo — *Akira* (1988), monitor close-ups
- Mamoru Oshii — *Ghost in the Shell* (1995)
- Signalnoise (James White) — contemporary retrowave poster design
- countless music-video and game-UI designers from the 2010s "synthwave" revival
