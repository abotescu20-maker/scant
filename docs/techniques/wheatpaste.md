# Wheatpaste

## Technique

A simple adhesive made from wheat flour (or starch) boiled with water, historically used to mount bills, posters, and handbills on walls. Adopted by street artists for paper-based, large-format works that would be impossible to hand-paint on-site.

Characteristic features:

- Printed poster (offset, screenprint, or photocopy) is brushed with paste and pressed to a wall
- Edges feather, wrinkle, or peel as the paper dries
- Weather degrades the work — fading, tearing, graffiti-over
- Layering over older posters creates the characteristic palimpsest of urban walls
- Visual texture of wrinkled, water-damaged paper is part of the aesthetic
