# Wind-Sway Animation

## Overview

Wind-sway animation is [[ghibli-studio|Studio Ghibli]]'s habitual use of moving grass, hair, fabric, and water surfaces as an emotional and atmospheric register, not merely decoration. Rather than freezing the environment around a character and animating only the figure, Ghibli scenes breathe: a hillside of susuki grass ripples, a shirt flaps, a sheet lifts off a laundry line, a cat's fur stirs. The technique is associated above all with [[hayao-miyazaki|Hayao Miyazaki]]'s direction.

## Method

- secondary animation passes devoted to wind-driven elements
- tall grasses drawn as clumps with offset oscillation timings
- hair and fabric with elastic overshoot and settle, following Disney-influenced inertia principles
- coordinated sound — the hiss of grass, the whip of cloth — with Joe Hisaishi scoring
- use of wind sequences at emotional pivots: revelation, loss, flight

## Characteristics

- environment never fully static, even in a "still" shot
- visible wind direction consistent across foreground, midground, background
- rhythmic wave of grass often running parallel to the frame horizontal
- scene-level atmosphere carried by motion rather than dialogue

## Why it matters for AI

A generated Ghibli still that freezes grass and hair into a rigid decorative mat loses the living quality of the aesthetic. Even in a single frame, directional wind — bent grass, lifted hair, a fluttering hem — signals the style.
