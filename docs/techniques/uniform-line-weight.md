# Uniform line weight

## Technique

Drawing convention where every outline — characters, props, architecture, distant background — is inked at the same thickness. The contour does not thin or swell to indicate light, perspective, or emphasis. This is the defining mark of [[ligne-claire|ligne claire]] and distinguishes it from most other comics traditions, which use weight variation for drama (American superhero brush-inking) or mood (manga speed-lines).

Consequences:
- every object reads with equal graphic importance
- depth conveyed by overlap, colour, and perspective, never by line
- drawings tolerate extreme reduction or enlargement
- children and adult readers parse the image at the same speed
- inked with technical pens (Rotring, Graphos) or fine sable brushes held at a constant angle
