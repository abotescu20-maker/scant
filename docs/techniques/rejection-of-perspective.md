# Rejection of Linear Perspective

## Overview

A compositional principle of [[persian-miniature|Persian miniature]] painting (and, in modified form, [[mughal-miniature|Mughal miniature]]) in which the European single-vanishing-point system is deliberately avoided. Rather than receding an image toward an illusionistic horizon, the painter constructs an elevated, tilted space where distant figures sit higher on the page rather than smaller, and every object may be shown from its most legible angle.

## Method

- tilted ground plane so that the viewer looks slightly down into the scene
- carpets, pools, and courtyards depicted almost flat, ornament fully visible
- buildings rendered with an axonometric-like projection — parallel lines remain parallel, no convergence
- figures stacked vertically to imply depth without shrinking
- scale determined by narrative importance and pictorial clarity, not optical distance
- text panels and architectural frames locate the image in the page rather than in imagined depth

## Why it matters for AI

A model that renders these scenes with Western perspective will "correct" the very feature that makes them Persian or Mughal. Preserving the flat carpet, the high horizon, the non-converging walls, and the vertically stacked groups is essential to the style.
